This is an interactive map of NERVA users around the world, tracking nodes in countries.

Frequently asked questions:


How does this work? Originally the data was pulled from a peer list. Since then angrywasp (Nerva developer) has implemented an anonymous analytics system built right into the Nerva daemon. This allows us to put together a comprehensive map of the network. The co-ordinates of each node IP are rounded off to prevent any individual from being identified, but typically GeoIP does not have a great deal of precision, and in rare cases will even detect the wrong country. So it is not possible for us to plot exact locations, only approximations.

Doesn't this compromise privacy? No personally identifiable information is being stored. A potential malicious actor could quite easily retrieve identifiable information (such as your IP address) from their node's peer list. If this is a concern, you should connect to the network through a VPN/Tor. Still, you can choose to opt out of the analytics by running nervad with the '--no-analytics' flag.

What are the custom datasets? Currently, data is retrieved from each node when it first starts. This means we do not detect when nodes go offline, thus some of the nodes in the default dataset (last 30 days) may no longer be active. Also, some nodes may have connected more than 30 days ago, and are still online, but wont appear on the map. I have decided that 30 days is a good cutoff point, but you can choose to filter to the last 7 or 14 days for a more recent snapshot of who has (re)connected to the network. Historical data is a snapshot of the preceding 30 days, taken every month. Dates are in standard format (not US).

What is probable origin? Nodes that geolocate to known co-ordinates of major datacentres (Google Cloud, etc) are flagged as cloud-hosted. It is reasonable to assume that most cloud-hosted nodes are actively mining. This statistic is a guesstimate only.
